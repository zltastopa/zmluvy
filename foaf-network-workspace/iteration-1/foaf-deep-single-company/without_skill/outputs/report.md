## 8. Key Findings and Risk Assessment

### Finding 1: STRIM-SK -- Same Person, Different Sector, CRZ Contracts
Milan Strelec, a long-standing director of PARA INVEST (construction, 863K EUR in CRZ), also wholly owns STRIM-SK s.r.o. (IT consulting, 71K EUR in CRZ). The two companies operate in entirely different sectors. While not inherently suspicious, this dual-sector presence with public contracts warrants attention to ensure no cross-subsidization or bid coordination.

### Finding 2: Signatory Overlap Red Flags
Two of three PARA INVEST contracts have signatory_overlap flags:
- Ing. Matej Vagac signs for 56 different suppliers (Stare Mesto contract)
- Mgr. Silvia Gruberova signs for 20 different suppliers (Handlova contract)
These indicate the use of shared legal representatives, which is common in Slovak public procurement but can mask coordinated bidding.

### Finding 3: Hidden Entities Flag
The Handlova contract (ZS Skolska ihrisko, 318K EUR) was flagged for "hidden_entities," suggesting potential undisclosed subcontractors or beneficial owners.

### Finding 4: Real Estate Development Cluster
The Kusalik family (core owners of PARA INVEST) is connected to multiple real estate development entities (Male Krasnany, Arboria Slnecna, Arboria Veterna, RegiusPark). Combined with PARA INVEST's construction capability, this suggests a vertically integrated construction-development business model.

### Finding 5: Buocik Network -- Broad but Historical
Mgr. Jan Buocik's connection to PARA INVEST is historical (former director from 2008). His current network is extremely broad (SLOVAKIA OFFICE SE, CULTUS Ruzinov, Louisville, OPTITECH Construction, law firms, civic organizations). His connection to CULTUS Ruzinov (67K EUR in CRZ, municipal cultural entity) suggests political/civic involvement. The OPTITECH Construction connection (prokurista since 17.02.2026) is notable given it is in the same sector as PARA INVEST.

### Overall Risk Level: MODERATE

The core PARA INVEST entity appears legitimate -- a construction company run by the Kusalik family with a consistent business profile and "vysoko spolahliv" (highly reliable) tax status. The main concerns are:
1. Milan Strelec's dual company presence in CRZ (construction + IT consulting)
2. Red flags on contracts (signatory overlaps, hidden entities, missing expiry dates)
3. The breadth of Jan Buocik's corporate network and his recent entry into OPTITECH Construction (same sector as PARA INVEST)
